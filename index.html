Neural Architecture & Processing Pipeline

CNN Architecture

Our system uses a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) to analyze images. As the AI scans deeper into the picture, it learns to recognize increasingly complex patterns—starting from simple edges all the way to spotting signs of melanoma.

Preventing Memorization

To ensure the AI actually 'learns' instead of just memorizing the training images (a problem called overfitting), we use techniques like Dropout and Batch Normalization. These force the network to become highly reliable at analyzing completely new, unseen skin lesions.

Adaptive Learning

The AI trains using an optimizer that automatically adjusts how fast it learns. If the model stops improving, a feature called ReduceLROnPlateau slows down the learning rate, helping the AI carefully fine-tune its accuracy.

Clinical Dataset & Preprocessing

Our network is trained on the HAM10000 dataset, providing a massive library of clinical images to ensure a high standard of medical analysis.

Data Handling Protocols

During training, the system uses Data Augmentation to create slight variations of the training images, making the AI much more robust in the real world. We specifically disabled vertical flips because skin lesions often have natural orientations that shouldn't be turned upside down. We also use Class Weights to ensure the AI prioritizes finding rare skin cancers equally, rather than being biased by common benign spots.
